History of OOP :-

1. Origins and Early Concepts
- **1960s:** The concept of OOP began to take shape with the development of Simula, created by Ole-Johan Dahl and Kristen Nygaard.
Simula introduced the idea of classes and objects, which became fundamental to OOP.

2. Smalltalk and Popularization
- **1970s:** Alan Kay, along with his team at Xerox PARC, developed Smalltalk.
Smalltalk was a complete implementation of OOP principles and heavily influenced future OOP languages.
Alan Kay is often credited with coining the term "object-oriented programming."

3. C++ and Mainstream Adoption
- **1980s:** Bjarne Stroustrup developed C++ by adding object-oriented features to the C programming language.
C++ made OOP more accessible to the programming community, as it could be used for both system programming and application development.

4. The Rise of Java
- **1990s:** Java, created by Sun Microsystems, became popular for its platform-independent approach, with a strong emphasis on OOP.
Java's slogan "Write Once, Run Anywhere" highlighted its portability,
and it played a significant role in popularizing OOP in enterprise and web development.

5. Modern OOP Languages
- **2000s and Beyond:** Many modern programming languages, such as Python, Ruby, C#, and Swift, adopted and enhanced OOP principles.
These languages introduced new features and paradigms,
making OOP more powerful and flexible.

6. OOP Today
- OOP remains a dominant paradigm in software development, widely used for its ability to model complex systems through objects,
encapsulation, inheritance, and polymorphism.
It continues to evolve with new languages and frameworks, adapting to the changing landscape of technology.
